Frequently Asked Questions


Q: What is the emphasis on social justice about?

College is a time and place to explore new things and expose ourselves to diverse issues, cultures, and people. Discussing what students may encounter during their college life will prepare them to face these issues with an open mind. This trip is intended to increase our awareness of the natural world, ourselves, our peers and, in a bigger picture, the interaction of people and their environment in other places.

Q: What if I have never been camping?

Don't worry. Many participants will have never been camping before. This is your opportunity to explore something new. Trip leaders will teach you all the basic skills you will need to be comfortable.

Q: What if I'm on medication?

A:Please list all medications (including inhalers) on your application. If you need assistance administering these prescriptions, please contact us so that we can make the appropriate accommodations. If you have a prescription for something not listed on your application (for example: you are diagnosed with strep throat 3 days before the trip), please bring a note from your doctor with you to campus.

Q: What if I have special dietary needs?

If you have specific dietary restrictions, please don't hestiate to contact us and tell us about them! We will try our best to accomodate your needs.

Q: Why does the Wilderness Project cost more than other Pre-Orientation Programs?

The program restration fee helps to cover a variety of expenses including food, leader trainings, gear acquisition, and transportation from the airport to campus and from campus to the woods.

Q: What about Financial Aid?

The Wilderness Project is dedicated to providng experiential education and outdoor recreation regardless of financial means. We offer two need-based scholarships. Please contact us if the program cost is prohibitive.

Q: When can I move in to my room?

We will return to campus by 2pm on August 21st. You can move in at this time. Anyone coming to help you move in should also arrive on campus at this time. All of your belongings besides your camping gear should be shipped or driven in to arrive on the 21st.

Q: Will I be able to move in to my dorm before we leave for the Wilderness Project?

The dorms will not be available for move-in when you arrive on campus. You can move into your dorm on August 25th at 2 pm when we return. Plan on bringing the essential items for the Wilderness Project and have the rest of your belongings shipped or brought to you on or after the 25th.

Q: Can I store belongings I don't need for the trip on campus while I'm on the Wilderness Project?

Please bring only what you need for the trip with you when you arrive on August 18th. You should arrange for the rest of your belongings to arrive on campus upon your return.

Q: How can my parents reach me if there is an emergency at home while I am on the trail?

Parents can call the Orientation Office from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm, Monday through Friday at (314) 935-8350. After-hours emergency contact information will be distributed to parents before freshman arrive on campus.
