We backpack. We rock climb. We explore.

The Wilderness Project (WP) is a Washington University Pre-orientation program that gives incoming students a chance to commit to the outdoor environment through backpacking and rock climbing trips. With other fellow newbies and the amazing group of student project leaders, students can form an open and tight community that will remain with you for the rest of their Wash U career.

WP also places emphasis on social and environmental justice, and promote active discussions of various topics ranging from class, gender, race, religion, and sexuality. Through these conversations, we build strong bonds with one another in an isolated natural environment, where we also learn important philosophies of environmental justice.


Backpacking or Rock Climbing?

BackPacking

Immerse yourself in the beautiful Ozarks. We will explore a portion of the Ozark Trail in southern Missouri in the Ozark Mountains. The trail that is approximately 24-miles consists of rocky ridges, quiet forest paths, and clear streams. The backpacking group will separate and take on two different trails: Blair Creek and Current River. While carrying your gear and food throughout the entire trip may be a challenge, the trails themselves is only moderately difficult, suitable for all skill levels. The backpacking trip will last 4 days and 3 nights. Check out the Ozark Trail webiste for more details.

Rock Climbing

Explore the peaceful and quiet Horseshoe Canyon Ranch(HCR). Climbers will set up camp for four days at HCR that offers a range of routes appropriate for climbers of all levels. Check out the HCR website for more details.
